Basic tags and such

nesting is when one element is inside another creating a parent child relationship. The child element is the element nested iniside.

One "parent" can have multiple children, therefore have multiple elements nested into one. This can be called a parent, child and sibling relationship

Elements nested inside of one parent (siblings), are at the same level.

It is recommeded to indent any child element by 2 spaces.

There is h1- h6 for headings and p is for paragraphs, unlike headers there are no numbers that follow.

you use em tags for italics and strong for bold. You can combine the two by nesting the two tags together like this.

em and strong give your text meaning outside of visual representaion


Strong tag is used for importance, seriousness, or urgancy

Caution! Fire-breathing dragon ahead!


b tags draw attention to a word or section of without conveying extra importance.

Luckily, Link had The Master Sword and used it to defeat the dragon and escape.


Em tag is used to stress emphasis on a piece of content in a way that changes the actual meaning of the content.

Further and further Link went into the cave. With each step, it grew darker. This was a very deep cave indeed


The i tag is used to convey a different mood or voice from the surrounding content. It's often used with foreign words or idiomatic phrases, technical terms, shp names, thoughts, ect.

As he traveled deeper into the cave, Link thought to himself, I hope Princess Zelda is in here.

Ordered and Unordered Lists

Unordered list is a bulleteed list in which order does not matter. It is used with the ul tag, with li tag for each item, such as a shopping list

  • Milk
  • Eggs
  • Bread
  • Cheese
  • Coffee

Ordered List are numbered and imply steps to be taken in order, such as a recipe or instructions to put ikea furniture together. It is the ol tag with the li tag inside as before

  1. get a box
  2. cut a hole in the box
  3. put the stuff in the box
